    Group Project

    Description
    ×


    The group project was divided into two sections: creating our studio and working with the client. Our studio is called "Multimedium," and we will be developing a project for the foundation called Stichting 18 September (18th September Foundation). This organization commemorates the liberation of Eindhoven during World War II.

    Roles:
    My role in this project will be as a front-end developer. I will be responsible for managing the Git repository and implementing website features. This role is well-suited to my background in software, allowing me to expand my skills significantly. Despite my primary role as a developer, I will actively participate in the research and prototyping phases, offering my assistance and skills to ensure the project stays on track.

    Topic:
    The project's focus is to create an interactive story portraying World War II events in Eindhoven. This interactive narrative will be a video format where users can make choices that influence the storyline. Resources, including videos and photos, will be supplied by the 18th September Foundation. Our primary stakeholder is the foundation, while our target audience is younger individuals aged 18-30. The goal is to engage and educate younger generations about World War II in an engaging and compelling manner.

    Tasks:
    Besides assisting with research and prototyping, my main responsibility will be to develop the website to its full functionality. My primary focus will be on ensuring the interactive story works seamlessly. This task involves writing code that manipulates timestamps, displays dialogue at the appropriate times, and transitions between scenes smoothly. It's a challenging task, but I'm prepared and excited to take it on.

    Plan:
    The main project will follow the Double Diamond method, which includes four phases: Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver. As for our studio, we have a tight timeline of three weeks to prepare our brand book. This brand book will outline our brand values, services, target group, typography, brand colors, and storytelling approach.

    Reflection:
    Overall, I'm very excited about this project. History is one of my hobbies and interests, so creating something around it that combines my passion for history with coding is very fulfilling for me. I'm particularly enthusiastic about presenting World War II events in an engaging way because I believe it's important for everyone to understand what happened during that time. This project is an opportunity to contribute to that understanding. Regarding our studio work, developing the studio's brand will definitely be an interesting experience. It's a chance to shape the identity of a project I'll be a part of, which adds another layer of excitement to the entire endeavor.

    Passionate Project

    Description
    ×

    Introduction:
    The Passionate project is the final assignment I'll be involved in this semester. Its objective was to select a media-related topic and thoroughly explore it. Students were given two options: to work individually or in groups, but regardless of the choice, each individual's work is graded separately based on their contribution.

    Roles:
    My colleagues and I have decided to collaborate on this project together, dividing roles based on our individual strengths and the outcomes we aim to achieve. In previous projects, I typically served as a front-end developer and managed the Git repository. However, this time, I've taken on the responsibility of conducting research alongside another colleague. Additionally, I will support the development team by addressing bug fixes and handling any Git-related issues that may arise, providing assistance wherever needed.

    Topic:
    The topic for this project builds on our studio work. This time, we will be developing our studio website to showcase information about us, our portfolio of work, the services we offer, and contact details. Our target audience will primarily be small indie companies. Our stakeholder for this project will be our teacher, Chris, with whom we will regularly share our progress and seek feedback.
    Tasks:
    Since I've been assigned the research responsibilities, I will focus on this aspect. I have divided the research methods with a colleague who shares this responsibility. My tasks include preparing interviews, surveys, and conducting a competitive analysis. Once these are completed, we will compile a comprehensive research document showcasing our findings and detailing our methods. Following the research phase, the remaining tasks will be handed over to the development team. They will be responsible for coding and ensuring the website is ready for production and launch.

    Structure:
    The plan for this project is to work in four phases: researching, prototyping, developing, and testing. Given the limited time available, this method will yield the best results and is manageable within our group.

    Reflection:
    Overall, I wouldn't say I'm particularly excited about conducting research this time, but I understand it's a necessary part of the project work, and I'm committed to giving it my all. I do like the topic we've chosen, and I believe our group will do very well.
